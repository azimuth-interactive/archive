OUR CLIENTS
 
 

Caroline Sykes - Postman (Scooby)
Caroline purchased Scooby in January 2002. They were consistent ribbon-winners in the 3'6" adult jumpers, with top ribbons at the Old Salem Charity Show, HITS/Catskill II, Saratoga Classic, Hunter Farm Jumping Derby and Princeton Classic, and made a successful foray into the low A/O jumpers in Vermont.

 
 

Caroline Sykes - Argentarius (Archie)
Caroline and Archie made the move from jumpers to hunters mid-year. Archie is learning how to go like a hunter and they should be formidable competitors in the A/O hunters in 2003.
 
 

Carol Traver - Diamond in the Rough (Lilly)
Carol is a longtime Riverstone client. Highlights for Carol and Lilly in 2002 included ribbons in M&S Adult Jumper Classics in Ocala and Vermont, a good showing at the M&S Adult Jumper Semi-Final at HITS/Catskills, and good ribbons at the Princeton Classic, HITS/Catskills and Vermont.

 
 
 

Ann Cohen - Got Milk (Mackie)
Ann had a successful year with her adult hunter Mackie, starting with ribbons in Ocala, moving on to consistent performances at HITS/Catskills and in Vermont. She's hoping that next year some of her red and yellow ribbons will be blue.
 

Betsy Simons - The Other Man (Tom)
With her spectacular horse Tom, Betsy earned great ribbons this year, including a reserve championship in the adult hunters at the Vermont Summer Festival.

 

Sarah Gill - G.I Joe (Joe)
Sarah shows her green Hanoverian, Joe in the adult jumpers. Sarah and Joe competed at the Hunter Farms Jumper Derby, in Vermont and at Riverstone's shows including the October Jumper Classic where they debuted successfully at 3'6."

 

Patty Mooney - Moving Target (Shorty)
Patty and Shorty made their jumper debut at Hunter Farm's Jumping Derby last spring. Patty had a great time and, schedule permitting, will come on the road with us a few times in 2003. Her daughter Colleen has come back to riding after taking a break to pursue gymnastics, and we're hoping to get her out there showing next summer too.
 

Elizabeth Regele - Rune
Elizabeth is currently leasing a jumper named Rune. She lives near the farm and is able to ride a lot. She shows in the jumpers on occasion, but mostly rides for fun.
 

Barbara Scuccimarra - Rubenson (Lil Ben)
Barb has been a Riverstone customer since the beginning. She has a young warmblood, Rubenzon (Lil Ben) who made a remarkable recovery from EPM as a two-year old and is just starting to jump. Barb's daughter Ellie, showed with Kim when she was a junior and we're trying to convince her to come with us next year too.
 

Pat Sorenson - Tsunami (Dave)
Pat is a long-time Riverstone client. She purchased Dave, a 17 hand bay TB gelding, several years ago. Dave is in his late teens and has some physical issues, so he's getting time off. Pat hopes to resume riding him in the spring.
 

Leah Langsam
Leah is a working student who went off to college this fall after a great year. She catch rode a few in Florida and then took the reins of TB mare I Love Lucy, who has taught a number of juniors the ropes in the jumpers. They were extremely successful over the summer and won top ribbons in M&S and NAL classics each week in Vermont. Leah came home for the Riverstone Jumper Classic and won the 3'6" classic with Lucy in October. She will help Kim manage "Riverstone on the road" during the summer of 2003.
 

Sophie Watkins - Solar Heat
Sophie took over the reins of her aunt's TB gelding Solar Heat (Sunny) this year. They were ribbon winners in Vermont in the 12 and under equitation and modified children's hunters. Sunny is an off-the-track TB and he's not always easy to ride, but Sophie is making great progress with him.
 

Rebecca Bliss
This is Becca's first year at Riverstone. She's a working student and last summer she showed E-mail (Elvis) at HITS and in Vermont. They won top ribbons in the children's hunters in great company. Becca is currently half-leasing another hunter, but she hopes to show Elvis again next summer.
 

Maggie Hales - Cover Girl (Maggie's Mare)
Maggie and her TB mare Maggie's Mare competed successfully in the children's hunters at home last summer earning a couple of championships. Maggie started college in the fall, but has been coming home to ride most weekends. She will move into the ranks of the adult hunters next year.
 

Laura Minieri - Amazing Grace
Laura, another new junior, and her little mare Amazing Grace (Gracie) came from a barn where she did dressage, some lower level eventing and was active in Pony Club. They are making the transition over to the jumpers while continuing with Pony Club.
 

Violet Collins - Savannah's Rainbeau (Rainy)
Eight-year-old Violet is a new Riverstone rider. She is leasing pony hunter Savannah's Rainbeau (Rainy) who is helping her learn to jump. She has a new pony arriving in the spring and we expect great things from the pair.
 

Samantha Perlman - Theodore (Theo)
Sam and her pony Theodore have been showing in the leadline from Ocala to Vermont for the past two summers. They certainly win the best looking award!
 

John Shepard - Ladite and E-mail (Elvis)
John owns Lion Hill Farm in Easton, Connecticut. Kim has consulted, trained and done clinics at Lion Hill and students from there show with us in the summers. Ladite is a nine-year-old Dutch warmblood stallion who has been at Riverstone since he was four. He has been showing in the jumpers for the past two years.
 

Dan and Nancy Paduano-Cavalier and Benny
The Paduanos are longtime Riverstone clients. Their sons Danny and John both showed with Kim when they were juniors. Nancy rode and showed in the hunters as well and Dan even rode for a bit. The Paduanos have two retired horses at the farm who are enjoying their freedom and turnout.
 

Eve Latiner - Rainy, Lucy and Fiddler
Eve is in college and is taking a break from riding, but her family still owns Rainy, Lucy and another large pony named Fiddler. They generously make their horses available for special lease situations at Kim's recommendation.
