Trainers and Staff
Kim Perlman owns and operates Riverstone, a 20-acre hunter-jumper facility in Garrison, New York, one hour north of New York City. He trains a mix of junior and adult clients who show at all levels from leadline to the A/O jumpers.
Under the watchful eye of George Morris and Hunterdon, Kim had a very successful junior career. In the early 1980s, he placed in the top 10 at the USET Medal Finals and the AHSA Medal Finals. After his junior career, he worked with Anne Kursinski. In 1984, Kim and his brother David established Wedgewood Farm, Inc., a successful show barn that competed around the country. Kim turned professional in 1984 and he and David trained juniors who qualified for and placed in all of the year-end equitation finals.
Kim came to Touchstone Farm as a trainer in 1990. In 1994 he purchased the business and changed the farm name to Riverstone. Today, Kim takes clients to shows throughout the Northeast in the spring, summer and fall including Old Salem, HITS, Hunter Farm, Saragota and the Vermont Summer Festival. In the winter, he takes a group to Ocala or Gulfport.
