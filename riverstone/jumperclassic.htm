Riverstone Jumper Classic
In 1998, the first Riverstone Jumper Classic was held at Riverstone. Its purpose was to help raise money to combat ALS (Lou Gehrig's Disease). Kim's wife Candy passed away in May 2002 after a long battle with ALS.
The show has become an annual mid-October event at Riverstone. It features the $10,000 Riverstone Mini Prix as well as a 3'6" child/adult jumper classic.
Competitors have ranged from clients showing in the 2'9" jumper class for the first time to former Olympians like Norman Dello Joio and Peter Leone. The 2002 winner was D'Accord, a young Selles Francais stallion owned by Newsprint Farm and ridden by Cian McDermott.
